Split-custody day — visual treatments

A "split" day is when the two kids end up with different parents at the same time. Today the lanes-with-hairline approach blends together. Below: two stronger options to compare. Each shows a 5-day strip with one split day in the middle, surrounded by normal solid-color days for context.

Patrick (mist) Danielle (cream) Ethan (E) Harrison (H)

Option 1

Diagonal split

The cell splits along a 45° diagonal: top-right wedge is one kid's parent color, bottom-left wedge is the other's. A 1-pixel ink line on the diagonal makes the split unambiguous. Each kid's letter sits in the corner that matches their parent, with the handoff time alongside.

Option 2

Diagonal stripes

Repeating 14-pixel diagonal bands of both parents' colors. The pattern itself signals "this day is different" — you can read it in your peripheral vision. Per-kid pills stack in the cell to spell out the mapping explicitly.
